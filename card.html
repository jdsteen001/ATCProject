Shiba Inu

The Shiba Inu is a Japanese breed of dog. The word "Shiba" in Japanese means brushwood, like the terrain over which the dog hunted. The word "Inu" means dog; the name "Shiba Inu" translates to Brushwood Dog.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus familiaris
  • Height: Males; 14"-17". Females; 13"-16"
  • Weight: 18lbs-23lbs
  • Original Use: Small game hunting dogs
  • Temperment: Spirited, loyal, bold, clever

The Shiba Inu is a unique breed of dog in that it has a lot of characteristics that are much more cat-like than dog-like. Its history as a hunting companion has made it very independent with an almost introverted personality. However, they are fiercely loyal dogs and when comfortable in their surroundings become much more outgoing and animated. Shiba's are extremely smart and clever, and are described as "escape artists." New owners are warned to properly fence in their yards, and purchase a harness in place of a regular collar. Shiba's can range in color from pure-white, pure-black, a mix of black & white, or different shades of brown. Most darker-fur Shiba Inu's will have some lighter areas in their coat.
